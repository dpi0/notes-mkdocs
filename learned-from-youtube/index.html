    Immanuel Kant's Moral Theory - a summary with examples¶

    https://youtu.be/V-j1uKrN-aU

    • Immanuel Kant's moral theory, never use a person as a mere means

    • utilitarianism states you must choose between the lesser evil/painful/bad action. this is different from that.

    • you should not act on a maxim that everyone around you potentially not agree to. this is kantian deontology. this is called categorical imperative i.e a general rule that applies to everyone everywhere

    • maxim: general intention. eating a sandwich, promising a date etc.

    • interaction with a bank teller, your get money she gets paid is fine. merely using teller to get an unacceptable thing is wrong. it is to involve a person in a scheme they they wouldn't agree to.

    • acting in a way to other person where if they of your intention, they would never agree to it. and this is wrong like fake promising to come on a date is wrong.

    • the intention of the person committing matters, if in general everyone could agree to their action, then it's fine.

    • Kant's theory make insincere promises unacceptable, utilitarianism says there nothing can't or can do it depends on the circumstances, choosing the lesser evil.

    • a tree has a purpose of being strong tall not flimsy thin because it's made out of stuff that makes it behave that way and its ought to behave that way.

    • things made out of Earth wind fire water.

    • modern view states that function is assigned to things by smarter beings that have a mind. humans give rock a purpose of a table, it doesn't have a built in one like Aristotle said.
